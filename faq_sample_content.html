All Loan Types

The Loan Overview page is designed to provide key summary information about your loan (credit, grant or trust fund), and is supported by detailed information on other pages of Client Connection. The two-column layout of the Loan Overview page is basically the same for all loans; however, there are some layout and line item differences depending on the financier, loan type, loan currency and whether retroactive financing is being provided.

The window opens with the amounts shown in the currency of commitment of the loan. You may change the currency to US dollars (USD), Euros (EUR), Japanese Yen (JPY) or your local currency by selecting the desired currency from the "Show Amounts In" dropdown list. Note that the amounts will be calculated using current exchange rates.

For IBRD Loans and IDA credits you may change the "as of" date for which the financial data is displayed .

In finance, a loan is the lending of money from one individual, organization or entity to another individual, organization or entity. A loan is a debt provided by an entity (organization or individual) to another entity at an interest rate, and evidenced by a promissory note which specifies, among other things, the principal amount of money borrowed, the interest rate the lender is charging, and date of repayment. A loan entails the reallocation of the subject asset(s) for a period of time, between the lender and the borrower
